Professional Summary

I am an embedded software developer with multiple project experiences. During my graduate studies, I researched AUTOSAR-related automotive component testing, drone sensor drift, and traffic simulation acceleration using CUDA.

In my previous role as a drone developer, I wrote control software that utilized Qualcomm RB5 devices and 5G modules for communication, GPS, IMU, and lidar sensors for SLAM-based localization and mapping, alongside AduPilot-compatible boards for flight.

Currently, I am working on set-top box (STB) development, focusing on Linux kernel debugging and device driver maintenance for several ARMv8-A-based STBs (KSTB5220, KSTB6150, KSTB4181, KSTB3220). I have also performed Dolby MS12 v1.3.4 and TB44 certification using the KSTB4181.

With my extensive experience in embedded systems, I am always eager to take on new development challenges.
